Onigiri

Image by 些細な日常 via wikimedia commons, licensed under ShareAlike 4.0 International

Description

A handheld snack, perfect on the go and easy to prepare ahead of time.

Onigiri is a triangle of sticky rice filled with anything from tuna, to eggs, to pickled plums. It's typically wrapped in seaweed.

The recipe below will outline the basics of making tuna mayo onigiri, but you can try it with other fillings.

Tools (and their substitutions)

  • rice cooker (sub - pot)
  • onigiri mold (sub - small bowl with plastic wrap)
  • small bowl of water
  • small rice paddle (sub - spoon)

Ingredients

  • 1 cup uncooked short-grain Japanese rice
  • kosher salt
  • 1 package nori sheets
  • 1 5oz can tuna in oil, drained
  • 1 Tbsp Kewpie mayonnaise
  • 1/2 tsp soy sauce

Steps

  1. Prepare and cook the rice. Be sure to wash and soak the rice to ensure proper stickiness prior to cooking. Let cool in a bowl until it can be handled comfortably.
  2. In a small bowl, combine tuna, kewpie mayonnaise, and soy sauce. This will be the filling.
  3. Rub hands with kosher salt.
  4. Dip fingertips in water throughout process. This will help to keep rice from sticking.
  5. Fill the bottom onigiri mold about halfway with rice.
  6. Make a small indent in the middle of the rice. Add a dollop of filling mixture.
  7. Fill the rest of the mold to the top with rice.
  8. Cover with the onigiri mold lid and press into shape.
  9. Release rice triangle from the mold and set aside. Repeat with remaining rice.
  10. Wrap rice ball in as much nori as preferred. Often this is a rectangular strip, or enough to cover the whole onigiri.
  11. (optional) Top onigiri with a small dollop of filling. This helps to identify what kind of filling it is.

Steps with Small Bowl

If you don't have an onigiri mold, you can make the rice balls this way instead!

  1. Place a piece of plastic wrap in the small bowl and sprinkle with salt.
  2. Add a 1/4-1/3c rice to the bowl.
  3. Make a small indent and add filling.
  4. Add an equal amount of rice on top to cover the filling. Sprinkle again with salt.
  5. Gather the plastic wrap together and wrap/twist tightly around the rice to form the ball. Release from plastic.
  6. Press the rice ball into shape with your hands.
  7. Follow same steps as above to wrap and top the rice ball.
