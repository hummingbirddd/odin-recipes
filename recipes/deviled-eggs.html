Deviled Eggs

Image by Marshall Astor via wikimedia commons, licensed under ShareAlike 2.0 Generic

Description

Deviled eggs - creamy, tangy, delightful little treats packed with flavor and happiness.
Why wait until Easter or Thanksgiving? This easy recipe will have you making deviled eggs all year round.
If you like a SOUR deviled egg, follow the recipe as-is. If you prefer a milder flavor, cut the vinegar and dijon in half at first and add more to taste.

Ingredients

  • 6 cooled and peeled hard boiled eggs
  • 2 Tbsp mayonnaise
  • 1 Tbsp white vinegar
  • 1 Tbsp dijon mustard
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • dash paprika
  • (optional) capers or parsley

Steps

  1. Slice the eggs in half lengthwise.
  2. Separate the yolks from the whites into a separate bowl.
  3. Break apart the yolks with a fork into small pieces.
  4. Add the mayonnaise, vinegar, and dijon mustard to the yolks.
  5. Mash and stir the yolk mixture together. The more you stir, the creamier your filling will be.
  6. Add salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Taste test. Add more of any ingredient based on your preference.
  8. Arrange whites onto serving dish.
  9. Add filling to piping bag or sandwich bag with corner cut off. Fill each white evenly. Squeeze excess directly into mouth to avoid over-filling whites.
  10. Sprinkle paprika over the top of all the eggs.
  11. Add optional toppings like capers (delicious) or parsley (pretty, but worthless).
  12. Consume.

Refrigerate any leftovers for up to two days... but let's be real, will you really have leftovers?
